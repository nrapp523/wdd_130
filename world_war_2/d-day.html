What was D-Day

D-day was an Allied invasion against the Axis Powers. It was done on June 6, 1944 at the beaches of Normandy, France. It was an assault which consisted of large scale attack via the air and sea.

The Turning Point

This was beginning of the end for the Axis powers. This invasion allowed the Allied forces to gain a stronger foothold in Europe, and put Germany on the defensive. This invasion is widely considered the beginning of the end of World War 2.
